**Puts table — column set and order.** Source: QQQ v2 sample Puts header:

```
| Strike | Bid | Ask | Mid | Delta | IV | Volume | OI |
```

Mid is computed as `(bid + ask) / 2`. Delta is signed (negative for puts). IV is one decimal `##.#%`. Volume and OI render with thousands separators.

**Strike selection rule.** For each trade leg, take its strike plus three strikes above and three strikes below from the chain snapshot's unique sorted strike list. Union across legs (so a two-leg spread yields up to 14 unique strikes, deduplicated). Sort ascending. Render leg rows in **bold** using markdown `**` around each cell.

After the table, emit the literal note:

**Strike-selection edge cases.**
- If the lower leg has fewer than three strikes below it in the snapshot, take what is available (don't pad).
- If the upper leg has fewer than three strikes above it, same.
- Always include both leg strikes in the table regardless of how many surrounding strikes exist.
- Strikes that fall between the two legs are always included (sample shows 710.00 between the 705 short and 715 long).

**Calls (context only) table — column set.** Source: QQQ v2 sample:

```
| Strike | Bid | Ask | Mid | Delta | IV |
```

No Volume, no OI. This is a sanity-check view of the call side near spot, not the trade leg side. Render three strikes straddling the current underlying spot:
- The strike closest to spot
- One strike above
- One strike below

If a strike-closest-to-spot calculation produces a tie (rare), pick the lower strike.
