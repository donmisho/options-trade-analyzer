## Relevant Context — Do Not Deviate Without Escalation

Source: `insight_engine.md` § 3.6 (gate mechanics)
There is NO "hard gate" / "soft gate" category in the schema. Gate behaviour is fully described by three
junction fields:
- `stop_if_fail = true` → on failure, halt the pipeline for that candidate and record.
- `stop_if_fail = false` → on failure, record and hold a `score_penalty`, then continue.
- `evaluation_order` → firing sequence within the rule's phase.

Source: `Scoring Parameters.xlsx` Sheet1 — map sheet categories to junction fields:
- Every **Hard Gate** row (Universal Pre-Filter, Per-Strategy, Trade Type) → `stop_if_fail = true`.
- Every **Soft Gate (Score Impact)** and **Post-Scoring** row → `stop_if_fail = false` with a
  `score_penalty` equal to the sheet's "REDUCE SCORE BY n":
  - Stock Extended (Against Entry / In Trade Direction) → penalty 10
  - SMA alignment against trade → penalty 15
  - Mixed chart signal on directional strategy → penalty 25
  - Cushion barely above ATR floor → penalty 5
  - ETF underlying is a BONUS (+5), modeled as a negative penalty / positive adjustment per
    `insight_engine.md` § 3.6 — keep its sign correct (increase, not reduce).

Source: OTA-684 description
- The **earnings gate** is `evaluation_order = 1` among the hard stops for SP / WG / TR, with
  `stop_if_fail = true`.
- Forward-looking pattern to capture in the junction `rationale` (NOT a new strategy here): a future
  180–360 DTE strategy's earnings-gate junction row would use `stop_if_fail = false, score_penalty = 0` —
  proving a future long-DTE strategy needs only junction rows, no code change. Record this in `rationale`.
- `evaluation_order` uniqueness within `(strategy, rule.phase)` is validated at engine load, not by a DB
  constraint — but you still set values that are unique within each strategy's phase.
