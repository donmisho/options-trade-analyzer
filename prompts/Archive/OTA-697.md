## Relevant Context — Do Not Deviate Without Escalation

Source: `insight_engine.md` §3 (Core concepts)
Rules:
- A **Candidate** is an opaque container of **named values** (§3.1) — `candidate_id`, `candidate_type` (opaque string), and a name→value map. The engine never invents named values; they come from the adapter.
- A **NamedValue** (§3.2) has a name, a **tier** (RAW · DERIVED · COMPUTED), a type (scalar number, enum, date, boolean), and null semantics.
- A **Rule** (§3.3) has: stable key, phase, condition expression and/or `formula_ref`, parameter schema (no values), tier, referenced named values, intent. Rules carry **no** per-strategy parameters, weights, or stop behaviour.
- A **Strategy** (§3.4) has: stable key, label, verdict band set, scan parameters, description. It carries **no** rule logic on its own.
- A **JunctionRow** (§3.4) is the only place a strategy's configuration for a rule lives: `enabled`, `evaluation_order`, `stop_if_fail`, `score_penalty`, parameter values, `weight` (scoring criteria only), `rationale`. There are no defaults inherited from the rule.

Source: `insight_engine.md` §3.5 / §3.6 (engine vocabulary — NOT domain terms)
Rule: Tier ∈ {RAW, DERIVED, COMPUTED}; phase ∈ {gate, scoring, adjustment, verdict}. These are fixed engine vocabulary and may be modeled as engine enums. Verdict **labels** (`EXECUTE`, `A`, `WAIT_FOR_EARNINGS`, …) are **opaque strings** the engine never interprets (§3.8). Named-value names, `candidate_type`, `consumer_surface`, and `strategy_key` are likewise opaque strings — never branch on their values.

Source: OTA-697 ticket — field-placement notes (as-built)
Rules:
- `evaluation_order` / `stop_if_fail` / `score_penalty` / `weight` are **junction inputs** → they live on `JunctionRow`.
- `terminal_phase` and `was_terminal` are **computed outputs** → they live on `ResultRecord` / `CandidateSnapshot` / `EvaluationDecision`, never on `JunctionRow`.
- `verdict` may be populated either by the Phase-7 band lookup **or** by a halting gate. The dataclasses must allow a non-null `verdict` on a halted, never-scored candidate (`final_score` null).

Source: `insight_engine.md` §4.2 (Result record) + §4.3 (bronze contract)
Rule: `ResultRecord` carries the **mandatory full per-rule trace** — every decision, including non-stopping and zero-penalty failures. `CandidateSnapshot` (one per candidate) and `EvaluationDecision` (one per rule evaluation) are the two logical persistence streams; they are **physical-store-agnostic** (no table name, no DB). The promote-to-column vs. `payload_json` split and provenance stamping are **OTA-704's** job — this Story defines the dataclass shapes only.

Source: OTA-695 OD-2 (terminal verdict from junction) + OTA-703 (verdict-source discriminator)
Rule: Anticipate two OD-resolved fields so downstream Stories populate rather than re-shape:
- `JunctionRow.terminal_verdict: str | None` — nullable halt verdict per binding (read by OTA-701, value-validated by OTA-699). Most rows are null.
- `ResultRecord.verdict_source` — one of `BAND_LOOKUP` | `HALT_TERMINAL_VERDICT` | `HALT_NO_VERDICT` (set by OTA-703). Define the field/enum now; population is downstream.

### Phase 1 — Implementation
1. Add the nine dataclasses (suggested home: `app/insight_engine/models.py`; a cohesive split into config / candidate / result modules is acceptable). Do **not** name any module `dataclasses.py` (stdlib shadow).
   - **`NamedValue`**, **`Candidate`**, **`Rule`**, **`Strategy`**, **`JunctionRow`**, **`RuleSet`**, **`ResultRecord`**, **`CandidateSnapshot`**, **`EvaluationDecision`**.
2. Model engine vocabulary as enums (e.g. `Tier`, `Phase`, `VerdictSource`); keep verdict labels, named-value names, `candidate_type`, `consumer_surface`, and `strategy_key` as opaque `str`.
3. Field requirements:
   - `Rule`: `rule_key`, `phase`, `tier`, `intent`, `condition_expression` (nullable), `formula_ref` (nullable), `referenced_named_values: list[str]`, `parameter_schema: dict`, `null_semantics`.
   - `Strategy`: `strategy_key`, `display_name`, `consumer_surface`, `description`, `compatible_structures: list[str] | None`, `verdict_band_set` (parsed), `dte_min`/`dte_max` (nullable), scan parameters.
   - `JunctionRow`: `evaluation_order`, `stop_if_fail`, `score_penalty` (nullable), `weight` (nullable), `parameters: dict`, `rationale`, `enabled`, `terminal_verdict: str | None`, plus the keys linking it to its rule and strategy.
   - `RuleSet`: the **resolved runtime view** — a `Strategy` plus its bound rules each paired with its `JunctionRow`, supporting phase-grouped access in `evaluation_order`. (Population/sorting is OTA-698; this Story defines the container that holds an ordered, phase-grouped binding set.)
   - `ResultRecord` (§4.2): `candidate_id`, `candidate_type`, `source_app_id`, `strategy_key`, `terminal_phase`, per-gate trace list (rule_key, phase, tier, `evaluation_order`, value(s) evaluated, params evaluated against, passed, `stop_if_fail`, `was_terminal`, held penalty, decision reason), per-criterion scoring breakdown (rule_key, raw value, weight, weighted contribution), `raw_score`, held penalties applied, per-adjustment results (rule_key, amount, condition triggered, score before/after, reason), `final_score` (nullable), `verdict` (nullable string), `verdict_source`, engine metadata (`engine_version`, `config_version` hash, `run_timestamp`).
   - `CandidateSnapshot` (§4.3): full named-value set + result-record summary, plus the logical provenance/identity fields (`snapshot_id`, `run_id`, `source_app_id`, `config_version`, `evaluated_at`, `candidate_type`, `strategy_key`, `symbol`, `final_score`, `verdict`, `terminal_phase`, `payload_version`). Provenance **stamping** is OTA-704; fields exist here.
   - `EvaluationDecision` (§4.3): correlation to its snapshot (`snapshot_id`, `run_id`), `rule_key`, `phase`, `tier`, `evaluation_order`, value evaluated, params, `passed`, `stop_if_fail`, `was_terminal`, `score_contribution`, decision reason, `payload_version`.
4. Prefer immutable (`frozen=True`) dataclasses for the config/resolved-config shapes (`NamedValue`, `Rule`, `Strategy`, `JunctionRow`, `RuleSet`); `Candidate` may keep a mutable named-value map so the adapter can populate COMPUTED values between phases (§5.2).
5. Extend `app/insight_engine/__init__.py` at OTA-696's marked extension point: import and add all nine dataclasses (and the public enums) to `__all__`.
